Managing Stress

Stress is a natural response to the demands of life, but chronic stress can negatively impact your health and well-being. Learning to manage stress effectively can improve your quality of life.

Understand Your Stressors

Identify situations, people, or responsibilities that trigger your stress. Self-awareness is the first step toward managing it.

Practice Relaxation Techniques

Methods like deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, meditation, and yoga can help you relax and reduce stress.

Stay Active

Physical activity boosts endorphins (feel-good hormones) and improves sleep and mood. Try walking, swimming, or dancing.

Maintain Healthy Habits

Sleep well, eat balanced meals, and avoid excessive caffeine or alcohol. These help your body cope with daily stressors better.
