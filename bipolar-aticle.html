Bipolar Disorder Basics

Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition marked by extreme shifts in mood, energy, and behavior. These shifts include emotional highs (mania) and lows (depression).

Types of Bipolar Disorder

  • Bipolar I: Manic episodes lasting at least 7 days or requiring hospitalization.
  • Bipolar II: A pattern of depressive and hypomanic episodes.
  • Cyclothymia: Frequent mood swings with milder symptoms.

Symptoms of Mania

  • Increased energy or activity
  • Decreased need for sleep
  • Racing thoughts
  • Impulsivity or risky behavior

Symptoms of Depression

  • Sadness or hopelessness
  • Fatigue
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Difficulty concentrating

Treatment Options

Bipolar disorder is managed through medication, therapy, lifestyle adjustments, and ongoing support. Early diagnosis and consistent care are essential.
