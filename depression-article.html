Understanding Depression

Depression is more than just feeling sad or having a bad day. It’s a serious mental health condition that can affect every aspect of your life. Recognizing the signs and understanding how to seek help is essential.

What is Depression?

Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. It can interfere with your daily functioning and relationships.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent low mood or sadness
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleep disturbances (too much or too little)
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Feelings of guilt, worthlessness, or helplessness
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Causes and Risk Factors

Depression can result from a complex interaction of social, psychological, and biological factors. Some contributors include:

  • Genetics and family history
  • Trauma or abuse
  • Chronic illness or medication side effects
  • Life events such as loss, stress, or isolation

Treatment and Support

Depression is treatable. Treatment options may include:

  • Psychotherapy (e.g., cognitive behavioral therapy)
  • Medication prescribed by a mental health professional
  • Lifestyle changes like regular exercise, sleep, and healthy eating
  • Joining a support group or talking to someone you trust

Seeking help is a sign of strength. You don’t have to go through depression alone—support is available.
