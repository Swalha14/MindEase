Understanding Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions that involve unhealthy relationships with food, weight, and body image. They can affect people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds.

Types of Eating Disorders

  • Anorexia Nervosa: Severe food restriction and intense fear of weight gain.
  • Bulimia Nervosa: Cycles of binge eating followed by purging behaviors.
  • Binge Eating Disorder: Frequent episodes of uncontrollable eating without purging.

Warning Signs

  • Obsession with weight, calories, or dieting
  • Extreme body dissatisfaction
  • Social withdrawal during meals
  • Fluctuations in weight

Getting Help

Treatment often involves medical care, therapy, nutritional counseling, and support from loved ones. Early intervention improves outcomes.

For professional guidance, visit NEDA - National Eating Disorders Association.
